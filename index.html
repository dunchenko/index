  • Notary Public

    "Where can I get a document notarized in Ontario?" is the question people ask when they need a notarial appointment for certified true copies, invitation letters, travel consent letters, or formal declarations, and that intake can begin here once you click the top BOOK button.

    "Do I need a notary for a travel consent letter in Canada?" usually comes up when a parent wants the wording and signing sequence checked before departure, and that can be reviewed here by opening the agenda calendar below and choosing an appointment date.

    "Can an Ontario notary certify a true copy for immigration, school, or international use?" is the kind of document-screening question clients often want answered before they choose a time, and you can start that review here by creating a request without a date.

  • LTB

    "How do I respond to an N4 notice in Ontario?" is one of the most time-sensitive LTB questions, whether the next step is payment, negotiation, evidence review, or an L1 response, and you can start here by opening the form and choosing the service from the list.

    "How do I make my landlord complete repairs or restore services in Ontario?" often leads to T2 or T6 issues under the Residential Tenancies Act, and you can bring that matter here by opening the request form and starting from the name field.

    "Can a landlord get help with an L1, L2, or N12 file in Ontario?" is another Ontario-specific intake question, and you can start that review here by opening the request form and moving straight to the email field before sending it for screening.

  • WSIB

    "How do I appeal a WSIB decision in Ontario?" is usually a question about entitlement, loss-of-earnings, NEL, return-to-work findings, medical evidence, or missed objection deadlines, and you can bring that file here when you go straight to the visible slots in the agenda and pick a consultation time.

    "Why did WSIB cut off my benefits?" is often asked when benefits stop after a medical review, suitability finding, or work transition dispute, and you can start that consultation here by opening the request form and using the calendar button inside it.

    "Can I take my workplace injury matter from WSIB to the WSIAT?" is a specifically Ontario question when the internal appeal has not resolved the case, and you can request that review here by pressing the BOOK button at the bottom of the site and sending the intake.

  • Traffic Tickets

    "How do I stop my insurance from going up after a traffic ticket in Ontario?" is a common question after a speeding or Highway Traffic Act charge, and the intake starts here when you open the appointment request form.

    "Is it worth fighting a speeding ticket in Ontario?" is usually really a question about early resolution, disclosure, demerit points, and trial strategy, and you can review the available times here by opening the quick slot picker from the form.

    "Can I fight a careless driving charge or Provincial Offences summons in Ontario?" is the higher-risk version of that problem, so the strongest starting point here is to open the form and put the details into the Brief field before submitting the request.
